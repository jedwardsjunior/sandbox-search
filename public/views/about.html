About Sandbox

Big Data. Simplified.

What is Sandbox?

Sandbox is a search engine for the deep web. In particular, Sandbox works to expose the knowledge and insights of big data analysis to the average Internet user.
The "deep web" refers to any content on the World Wide Web that is not discoverable by traditional search techniques such as text-matching. While this includes password protected content, Sandbox is focusing on tapping into publicly available data sets and web content to more effectively answer our users' queries.

How is Sandbox different than Google?

While Google's knowledge boxes can be similar to the type of results that Sandbox provides to its users, Sandbox differs in that it executes its search primarily by accessing various APIs and big data sets, not just by looking for matching text on a webpage.
To do this, Sandbox utilizes publicly available APIs of major producers of Internet content, like Wikipedia, Facebook, and Twitter. It also currently accesses the APIs of other computational search engines, like Wolfram Alpha. In the near future, Sandbox will also incorporate publicly available big data sets into the search process.

Mission

Sandbox aims to place the power of big data analysis at our users' fingertips. There is so much more we could learn from the Internet, and Sandbox strives to make this information more accessible and easier to understand.
